# Executive Summary

This project analyzes **customer behavior across the e-commerce lifecycle** using interaction data from an online retail platform.

The objective is to understand how users move through the shopping journey and identify opportunities to improve **conversion, engagement, and retention**.

---

# Dataset Overview

The dataset contains:

• **1,999,840 user interaction events**
• **935,717 unique users**

Each event represents one of three actions:

* viewing a product
* adding a product to cart
* completing a purchase

---

# Key Findings

### Conversion Funnel

The majority of platform activity occurs at the **product view stage**.

A significant drop-off occurs between:

```
Product View → Add to Cart
```

However, once users add items to their cart, the likelihood of completing a purchase increases significantly.

---

### User Engagement

User engagement metrics indicate moderate platform interaction:

• **Average sessions per user:** 1.68
• **Same-day repeat users:** 29.97%
• **7-day return rate:** 16.28%

This suggests that many users interact with the platform briefly but do not consistently return.

---

### Customer Segmentation

User behavior varies significantly across different segments:

• Browsers who primarily view products
• Cart users who demonstrate purchase intent
• Purchasers who generate direct revenue
• Low engagement users with minimal interaction

Understanding these segments enables targeted strategies for improving conversion and retention.

---

# Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, several strategic opportunities were identified:

• Improve product page experience to increase **view-to-cart conversion**

• Introduce **personalized product recommendations**

• Implement **targeted promotions for cart users**

• Strengthen **retention strategies** through remarketing and loyalty programs

---

# Project Impact

This lifecycle analysis provides insights that can help e-commerce platforms:

* increase conversion rates
* improve customer retention
* personalize marketing strategies
* optimize product discovery

These insights demonstrate how behavioral analytics can support **data-driven decision making in digital commerce**.
