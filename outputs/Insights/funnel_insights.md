# Funnel Analysis Insights

## Overview

The funnel analysis examines how users progress through the e-commerce shopping journey:

View → Cart → Purchase

The goal is to identify where the largest drop-offs occur and where the business can improve conversion performance.

## Key Findings

• The majority of platform activity occurs at the **product view stage**.

• Only a small proportion of users move from **viewing products to adding them to cart**, indicating that the largest drop-off occurs between **product exploration and purchase intent**.

• Once users add products to the cart, the likelihood of completing a purchase increases significantly.

## Business Interpretation

The product page stage appears to be the main friction point in the customer journey.

Users show interest by viewing products, but many do not proceed to cart.

Possible causes may include:

- insufficient product information
- weak product recommendations
- pricing sensitivity
- lack of urgency or promotions

## Recommendations

• Improve **product page experience** (images, descriptions, reviews)

• Add stronger **"Add to Cart" call-to-action**

• Implement **personalized recommendations**

• Test **limited-time promotions or discounts**

Improving the view-to-cart transition can significantly increase overall conversion.
